The life cycle assessment module is straightforward and computationally cheap,
but the capacity expansion model consists of a large mixed-integer linear program that is computationally expensive and depends on time-series input data.
Three methods are proposed with varying tradeoffs between accuracy, CEM computational cost, optimization computational cost, and development time:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Create a nonlinear surrogate model of the CEM for design optimization by performing an offline brute-force sweep of the CEM for various WEC designs, leveraging a reduced-order WEC dynamics model to decrease the number of CEM runs.
\item Create a simple linear surrogate model of the CEM for design optimization by performing an offline singular run of the CEM for a grid without WECs (price taker assumption), combined with dual sensitivities to obtain marginal economic and environmental prices of energy and capacity.
\item Use the CEM as-is within the design optimization.
\end{enumerate}
