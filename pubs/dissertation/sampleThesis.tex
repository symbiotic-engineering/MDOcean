\begin{biosketch}
Becca is a 5th-year Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering interested in the design of renewable energy systems.
She enjoys solving integrated mechanical, electromagnetic, fluid, and controls problems through simulation, systems design, and prototyping.
Her PhD research at Cornell's Symbiotic Engineering and Analysis Lab with Prof. Maha Haji involves 
optimizing ocean wave energy toward economic viability and reduced carbon emissions. 
Outside of research, Becca enjoys distance running, working with engineering for social impact organizations, and neurodiversity advocacy.
During her PhD, she completed three half-marathons and founded and led two student organizations: 
GAIA (Global Action Impact Association) and SNAC (Student Neurodiversity Alliance at Cornell).
Becca graduated from MIT in 2021 with a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering. 
At MIT, she focused on electric vehicles, writing her bachelor's thesis on electric motor optimization for Formula SAE cars. 
\end{biosketch}
